Coaching Principles

A coach approach is believing and acting in a way that supports people to thrive and reach their full potential. This shows up in the coaching principles.

IRBC Highlight

Many of the coaching principles align with the Indigenous relations behavioural competencies (IRBC). As you review the coaching principles, reflect on how they connect with the IRBCs.

Supporting others to come up with their own solutions instead of solving their problems empowers them.

Try it! Ask what they’ve done before that’s worked instead of telling them what to do.

When a person knows that you’re fully present by listening deeply and without judgement, it encourages them to be honest and vulnerable.

Try it! Set distractions aside, listen to what the other person said, then ask an open question relevant to what was just said.

When we’re curious, open, and able to suspend our judgment, people are more likely to offer their ideas and perspective.

Try it! Be aware of your assumptions and put them aside to listen to what the other person is saying and ask questions to understand more.

If a person feels responsible for something, there is a sense of commitment and personal accountability for the result.

Actionable Insight: Ask: “What actions could you take next time to ensure a different result?”

Being open to the possibilities explored rather than sticking to your point of view helps others believe in themselves and sets the stage for possibility, innovation, and potential to grow.

Actionable Insight: Ask: “Let’s keep an open mind. What other possibilities are there?”

Support means working alongside people to help them get what they need. Challenge means encouraging their growth and accountability with respect and empathy. Not sure what’s needed? Stop guessing and start asking.

Actionable Insight: Ask: “How can I best support you right now? Where do you think a challenge might help you grow?”

Bring your strengths and who you are. Being authentic as a coach is also about being genuine about your intentions.

Actionable Insight: It can be challenging to be authentic all the time. Check in with yourself: What do they need and am I able to show up in the way I need to? e.g., saying “no” when you don’t have the capacity to coach in the moment.
