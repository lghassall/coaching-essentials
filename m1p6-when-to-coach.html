When to Coach (and when not to!)

Just like a trail guide, you wouldn’t guide someone on every step. Sometimes it’s faster and safer to point the way! The same goes for coaching - it's not always the best approach.

Think of communication on a spectrum, from telling people what to do (directive) to working with them to find their own solutions (non-directive). When you have a challenge and approach someone for help, do you prefer a direct approach? (just tell me what to do!) or a coaching approach? (work with me to find solutions). You might say: It depends on the situation.

Coaching isn't a one-size-fits-all approach. You need to decide whether it makes more sense to take a coaching approach, a directive approach, or something in between. There’s no right or wrong, but some approaches will be more effective than others.

“Coaching isn’t about trying to change someone’s behaviour by telling them what to do. We have to be honest with ourselves and the other person about the approach we’re taking, because if we call it coaching but are really directing, people will sense the disconnect and trust can erode. Before any conversation, clarify your intention: What does this person need right now: direction or coaching?”
– Certified Coach
