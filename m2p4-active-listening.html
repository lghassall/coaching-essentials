Elements of Active Listening

Active listening is more than just simply hearing someone. It’s an intentional effort to fully understand their message. Below are five elements that are key to creating connecting and understanding in conversations and how you can put them into action.

Optional: Start with watching The Art of Active Listening | Amy Gallo | HBR | 7:39 video

1. Seek to Understand

  • Pause before replying
  • Listen to their words, tone, and emotions without interrupting
  • Ask open-ended questions to gain understanding
  • Don’t jump to conclusions or fix things

2. Respect perspectives and attributes

  • Respect their unique experiences, values, and choices
  • Suspend judgment and create space for diverse viewpoints
  • Resist jumping to conclusions or projecting your own experiences onto theirs

3. Connect with empathy, support and concern

  • Demonstrate genuine care through presence and compassion
  • Focus on their feelings and meaning, not your next line
  • Validate their experience before offering advice or perspective
  • Acknowledge challenges and celebrate progress

Connection | IRBC

Empathy is the ability to recognize, understand and directly experience the emotion of another. It involves listening with heart, accepting their message and staying focused on their experience rather than reacting.

4. Build trust with openness and transparency

  • Be authentic and consistent in communication and behaviour
  • Share intentions, boundaries, and observations honestly

5. Reflect and clarify for understanding

  • Summarize or paraphrase key points to confirm shared meaning: “So what I’m hearing is…, is that accurate?”
  • Invite them to say more e.g., “tell me more…”

Connection | IRBC

Open listening means listening to and valuing the telling of stories and letting pauses in conversation extend into silence rather than jumping into dispute, agree, question or move on.
