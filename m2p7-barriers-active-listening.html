Barriers Active to Listening

Distractions

In today’s busy world, distractions are constant, and being truly present takes effort. We all have a lot going on, and our time and presence can be such a gift. Small steps, like silencing your phone, turning off notifications, letting others know you’re unavailable, or finding a quiet spot, can make a big difference in how deeply we connect and communicate with others.

2. “Triggers” or Activators

Life would be so simple if we had total control of what happened moment by moment in our lives and could remain fully present. But the reality is that life is full of unexpected events, and many of these events and situations may trigger or activate us. We can also get activated in a coaching conversation, which can get in the way of presence and active listening. This happens from time to time and it’s normal.

3. Unconscious Bias

We all bring biases to work. While that can help us quickly process and organize large amounts of information, conscious or unconscious bias can also lead us to make assumptions about people and then act based on those assumptions.

Because biases are shaped by instinctive feelings and worldviews as well as rational thought processes, we may not always be aware of their influence on everything we think, feel and do.

Examples in coaching might be assumptions about capability or potential, unequal opportunities for advancement or visibility, or connecting more easily with those that share your background or communication style, which could influence how the conversations unfolds.

Reflection

What’s the number one thing that gets in the way of your active listening? How would you like it to be different? What steps could you take to make it that way?
