Interrupting Barriers to Active Listening

The good news is that we can learn to disrupt barriers to active listening, making sure they don’t get in the way of being present and using active listening as much as possible. Self-awareness is fundamental to understanding ourselves, our interactions with others and our ability to be fully present and create a psychologically safe environment.

"While 95% of people think they’re self-aware, only 10-15% truly are."

Tasha Eurich, author of Insight

Connection | IRBC

Self-Discovery and Awareness is an Indigenous relations behavioural competency that applies to all BC Public Service employees, no matter where you work. It means understanding one's thoughts, feelings, values and background and how they impact the success of the interaction and relationship, or how they may influence one's work.

Managing your reactions

If we can identify when we’re activated, we can pause, take notice, and choose to respond differently. Becoming more aware of when we’re feeling activated takes time and practice!

Navigate through the flashcards below to learn about things you can do:

Card 1 of 4: Notice Feelings and Reactions

Notice when you're feeling activated or triggered and how you react: e.g., avoidance, appeasement, anger, upset, shut down, etc.

Card 2 of 4: Label Reactions Normal

Label your reaction as normal. We all have emotional triggers/activators.

Card 3 of 4: Notice Reactions

Without judgement, notice if you always choose the same reaction and how much it impacts you and your presence.

Card 4 of 4: Choose a Different Way

Choose a different way e.g., breathe in, breathe out, focus on curiosity, share how you’re feeling, or stay calm and do nothing.

What can you do to disrupt bias?

Reflect:

  • What assumptions am I making instead of asking?
  • Have I given this person the same benefit of the doubt I give others? Am I equally curious and open-minded?
  • Am I listening to understand what’s at the heart of their communication, even if their communication style differs from mine?
  • What might I learn if I paused and listened a little longer?
