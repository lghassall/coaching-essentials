What makes a question powerful?

Powerful questions are...

  1. Open-ended

    e.g., need more than a simple “yes” or “no”. e.g., Were you successful?

  2. Start with “what” to focus on possibilities

    e.g., What does success mean to you?

  3. Avoid “could”, “would", “should” (and "why" in some cases)

    These can seem judgemental and can shut down conversations e.g., What should you have done?

  4. Use silence

    Silence can be uncomfortable for some people, and they want to fill the space with words. Use pauses and silence to create space for the other person to expand and allow for reflection and discovery.

  5. Are short

    Short questions are easier to understand, encourage thinking, and promote conversation.

  6. Asked one at a time

    Ask a question and then wait for the response rather than asking a string of questions. It’s easier to understand and respond to.

  7. Avoid leading

    A leading question subtly (or not so subtly) pushes the person to respond in a specific way. e.g., “How frustrating was that experience for you?” vs. “How was that experience for you?”

Examples:

  • What’s standing in your way?
  • What will happen if you don’t take this step?
  • What does success look like?
  • What have you done to try to solve the problem?
  • What do you want to change?
  • What do you need most right now?
  • What’s important about that for you?
