Key Elements of Powerful Feedback and Acknowledgments

Effective feedback and acknowledgements go deeper than a compliment such as “well done”. It connects to what’s important to the person, their character and values, and celebrates their internal strengths.

Listening to understand gives you the ability to pick out what is important. It’s rare for people to feel seen and understood in this way. Through this type of connection, we build trust-based relationships.

Key Elements of Powerful Feedback and Acknowledgments

There are six key elements of powerful feedback and acknowledgements. Use the chevron buttons or the left / right arrow keys to navigate through the cards

Card 1 of 6: Observed

Based on something you noticed.

Card 2 of 6: Specific

Concrete about what you’re acknowledging and avoids vague statements.

Card 3 of 6: Neutral

Focused on effort, process, or experience without labeling. e.g., “I noticed you spent extra time organizing the data tables for the report.”

Card 4 of 6: Sincere

Genuine recognition that reflects your true appreciation.

Card 5 of 6: Timely

Acknowledgement given soon after the behaviour is observed.

Card 6 of 6: Impact‑oriented

Highlighting the effect of their effort on you, the team, or the project. e.g., “Your careful review of the draft helped the team meet the deadline and improved clarity for everyone.”

Compliments vs. Acknowledgements

There’s a difference between a compliment and an acknowledgement. An acknowledgement is specific, neutral, and impact oriented.
